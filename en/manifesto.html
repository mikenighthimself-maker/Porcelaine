Manifesto

Porcelaine is a maison that chooses restraint as its voice. We design services meant to endure, to be passed on, to accompany tables that settle into time. Our pieces do not seek effect; they pursue presence, balance, and the calm of a form that rests with confidence.

A porcelain maison

Each collection is born from a dialogue between hand and material. Porcelain is demanding, nearly uncompromising. It insists on exactness, on the discipline of fire, on the patience of repeated trials. We embrace that rigor because it creates a slower rhythm and a clearer attention. In the atelier, light reveals the proportions, the finely resolved lips, the quiet architecture of each silhouette.

Circle as structure

Our principal collection carries a simple name: Cercle. The circle is a fundamental form, an unobtrusive architecture that gathers. It frames the meal, orders the space, and brings people closer. In every plate and bowl we seek the exact alignment between thickness, curve, and material. Nothing is ornamental; everything is structural, everything is proportion.

Domestic rituals

We believe that the rituals of the home are a form of culture. Serving lunch, pouring tea, setting a table — these quiet gestures deserve objects that are attentive. Distinction does not need to be spoken; it is set, silently, in the clarity of a mineral white, in the softness of a rim beneath the hand, in the stability of a well-composed table.

Permanence and responsibility

Our series are short, our editions controlled. We privilege French partners who share a concern for craft and transmission. A Porcelaine piece is made to cross years without losing its clarity. It must resist, settle into a home, and remain beautiful after hundreds of meals. It is a form of responsibility: produce less, produce better, choose better.

Porcelaine speaks to those who choose permanence over immediacy. We defend a quiet beauty, never declarative. Distinction is never spoken, only set — piece by piece — into everyday life.
